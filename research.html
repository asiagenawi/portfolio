Jan 2024 – Sep 2025

Software Development Intern

Project-8 Experiment, Yale University Wright Laboratory

Built containerized HPC data-processing pipelines in Python/C++ using Globus CLI for secure parallel data transfers across 17 distributed sites. Implemented magnetic-field optimization workflows reducing electron-signal inhomogeneity artifacts by ~80%.

My experience researching for Project 8 was nothing short of phenomenal. In just a short period of time, I had learned so much about not only Neutrino physics, but myself and my interests. After the program at UIUC had ended, I felt eager to continue my work.

As a high school junior with little research experience, I knew it was a long shot, but I began cold emailing researchers in the consortium, asking to intern under their guidance. To my surprise, Dr. Hamish Robertson (University of Washington) and Dr. Penny Slocum (Yale) responded and so graciously welcomed me onto their projects. To this day, their willingness to commit their time and energy to my growth is one I will eternally be grateful for.

I attribute much of my technical and research skills to my experience @ Project 8. This collaborative environment pushed me to take on increasingly technical responsibilities and deepen my role as a research software contributor.
